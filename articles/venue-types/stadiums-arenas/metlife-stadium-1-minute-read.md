# MetLife Stadium Just Lost Its Name for the World Cup

The MetLife signs are gone. As of April 8, workers draped covers over the familiar branding atop the East Rutherford stadium, rechristening it "New York New Jersey Stadium" ahead of the 2026 FIFA World Cup.

Why? FIFA's "clean stadium" policy bans any corporate branding that isn't from an official FIFA sponsor. Fourteen of the 16 host venues across the U.S., Mexico, and Canada are getting the same treatment — Estadio Azteca becomes "Mexico City Stadium," Levi's Stadium becomes "San Francisco Bay Area Stadium," and so on.

The stadium isn't just getting a new name. It's been renovated over the past two years — four corners of the lower bowl were demolished and rebuilt to widen the field for FIFA-regulation grass. And in a move that will horrify tailgating purists, parking is completely banned on match days. Fans will ride NJ Transit from Penn Station instead.

Eight matches are coming to East Rutherford this summer, including heavyweights like Brazil vs. Morocco, France vs. Senegal, and England vs. Panama — all building toward the World Cup Final on July 19. Over a million visitors are expected in the region, with an estimated $3.3 billion economic impact.

The MetLife signs will probably go back up eventually. But for one summer, the stadium in the Meadowlands belongs to the world.
